The real challenge

What farmers, vendors & buyers face daily

Uganda's agriculture sector generates $4.7B annually yet 60% of farmers earn below the poverty line. AgriBridge addresses six critical failures.

68%📉

Price Fluctuations

Tomato prices crash from UGX 8,000 to UGX 1,500/kg at harvest — farmers lose 80% of income while buyers still pay high retail prices. No price transparency exists between supply and demand.

45%🔗

Middlemen & Inaccessibility

45% of farm produce never reaches consumers profitably. 3–5 middlemen each add 20–40% margins, making food expensive for buyers and worthless for farmers. No direct connection exists.

30%🕐

Post-Harvest Loss & Timing

30% of Uganda's fresh produce rots before reaching market. No platform tells farmers when to harvest, when demand peaks, or connects them to buyers in real-time to prevent wastage.

72%🏨

Hotels & Vendors Unserved

Hotels, restaurants and supermarkets in Kampala struggle with inconsistent supply. 72% have no direct farmer relationship and depend entirely on unreliable market intermediaries.

85%📋

No Farmer Profiling

85% of Ugandan farmers are unknown to formal buyers. Without verified profiles, credit history, or production records, farmers cannot access finance, forward contracts, or premium markets.

90%⭐

Quality Uncertainty

Buyers have no quality guarantee. 90% of bulk buyers report receiving substandard quality at least monthly, with no recourse, no rating system, and no consistency guarantee for repeat orders.
